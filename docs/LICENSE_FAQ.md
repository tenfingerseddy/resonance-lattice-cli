## Can I use this in my own developer workflow?

Yes. Internal use in developer workflows is allowed, including local development, CI/CD, testing, evaluation, coding assistants, automation, and internal engineering systems.

Yes. You may use this project as a tool, dependency, or input in your own development workflow. What you may not do without a commercial licence is offer this project itself, or a modified version of it, as a competing hosted, embedded, or redistributed product or service.

## Can I use it inside my company in production?

Yes, for internal business use.

## Can I use it in an internal LLM assistant or agent?

Yes, provided the assistant or agent is for internal use and you are not offering the Licensed Work itself to third parties as a competing product or service.

## Can I ship my own product that was built using this project?

Yes, if your product does not include, embed, redistribute, or provide the Licensed Work itself as a substantial part of a competing commercial offering.

Yes, subject to this licence. Modified versions and derivative works remain governed by this licence until the applicable Change Date for that version.

## Can I offer this as SaaS or an API to customers?

Not without a separate commercial licence if the service provides a substantial part of the Licensed Work's functionality.

## Can I embed this project in my commercial product?

Not without a separate commercial licence if the Licensed Work provides a substantial part of the core functionality of that product.

## Are my prompts, data, and outputs mine?

Yes. This licence is about the Licensed Work itself. It does not claim ownership over your own prompts, data, or outputs.

## Does this become open source later?

Yes. Each version converts to the Change License (Mozilla Public License 2.0) on the Change Date stated in the licence — four years from the date that version was first publicly released.

## Why use this licence?

The goal is to let developers and organisations use the project freely in real workflows, while preventing direct commercial cloning of the project itself.
